# RBI Assistant & Grade B – Prelims Syllabus (with sub-topics)

*Reference books: SP Bakshi (Objective General English, Arihant), RS Aggarwal (Quantitative Aptitude, S. Chand), Fast Track Objective Arithmetic (Rajesh Varma, Arihant), BS Sijwali (A New Approach to Reasoning, Arihant).*

---

## RBI Assistant English Syllabus

| #  | Topic                   | Sub-topics (from SP Bakshi / exam pattern)                                                  |
|----|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | Comprehension           | Reading comprehension, objective comprehension, inference, main idea, vocabulary in context |
| 2  | Subject Verb Agreement  | Number, person, collective nouns, indefinite pronouns, compound subjects                    |
| 3  | Cloze Test              | Fill in the blanks (single/multiple), context-based word choice                             |
| 4  | Sentence Rearrangement  | Jumbled sentences/paragraphs, sequence of sentences, connectors                             |
| 5  | Tenses                  | Present, past, future (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous)                     |
| 6  | Synonyms & Antonyms     | Vocabulary, word meaning, contextual usage                                                  |
| 7  | Spelling Correction     | Commonly misspelt words, spelling rules                                                     |
| 8  | One-word Substitution   | Single word for phrase, foreign words and expressions                                       |
| 9  | Sentence Completion     | Fillers, sentence improvement, selecting correct sentence                                   |
| 10 | Direct & Indirect Speech| Narration, reported speech, transformation                                                  |

**Grammar (SP Bakshi Part A & D):** Articles, Modals, Parts of Speech, Active & Passive Voice, Kinds of Sentences and Clauses, Spotting Errors, Punctuation, Contractions.

**Verbal (SP Bakshi Part B):** Paronyms & Homonyms, Idioms & Phrases, Sentence Completion.

## RBI Assistant Numerical Ability Syllabus

| #  | Topic              | Sub-topics (from RS Aggarwal / Fast Track Arithmetic / exam pattern)                 |
|----|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | Profit and Loss    | CP, SP, profit/loss %, discount, marked price, successive discount                   |
| 2  | Data Interpretation| Tables, bar graphs, pie charts, line graphs, caselets, mixed DI                      |
| 3  | Quadratic Equation | Roots, comparison of roots, condition for roots, word problems                       |
| 4  | Number Series      | Wrong number series, missing number, pattern-based series                            |
| 5  | Algebra            | Linear equations, identities, simplification of expressions                          |
| 6  | Averages           | Mean, weighted average, age-based problems                                           |
| 7  | Percentages        | Conversion, increase/decrease, population, application                               |
| 8  | Mensuration 2D     | Area and perimeter of square, rectangle, circle, triangle, parallelogram             |
| 9  | Interest           | Simple interest, compound interest, instalments                                      |

**Additional (RS Aggarwal / syllabus):** Simplification (BODMAS, brackets, surds, decimals), Ratio & Proportion, Time & Work, Time & Distance, Boats & Streams, Pipes & Cisterns, Number System, HCF & LCM, Problem on Ages, Partnership, Data Sufficiency.

## RBI Assistant Reasoning Syllabus

| #  | Topic                | Sub-topics (from BS Sijwali / exam pattern)                                    |
|----|----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | Odd Man Out          | Classification, verbal and non-verbal, figure series                           |
| 2  | Blood Relation       | Family tree, coded relations, generation-based                                 |
| 3  | Coding and Decoding  | Letter/number coding, conditional coding, matrix coding                        |
| 4  | Analogy              | Verbal analogy, alphabetical analogy, number analogy                           |
| 5  | Direction Based Qs   | Direction sense test, distance, shadow-based                                   |
| 6  | Seating Arrangements | Linear, circular, rectangular, facing in/out, order-based                      |
| 7  | Symbols              | Symbol substitution, symbol notation, inequalities                             |
| 8  | Number Series        | Numerical series, letter series, alphanumeric                                  |

**Verbal (BS Sijwali):** Syllogism, Logic-based Venn diagrams, Input-Output, Clocks, Calendar, Statement & Arguments, Statement & Assumptions, Course of Action, Cause & Effect, Decision Making.

**Puzzles:** Order ranking, scheduling, floor-based, box-based, comparison.

**Non-Verbal (if applicable):** Figure analogy, classification, series, cubes and dice, mirror/water images, embedded figures.
